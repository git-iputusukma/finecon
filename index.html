Timeline Themes Paradigm Foundation

The Modern Finance Valuation based on rational economic behavior Optimization (Markowitz), irrelevance of capital structure (Miller & Modigliani), irrelevance of dividend policy (Modigliani & Miller), CAPM (Sharpe, Lintner, Mossin), efficient market (Fama) Financial economics
The New Finance Inefficient market Inductive ad hoc expected return (Haugen), factor models (Chen, Roll, & Ross), behavioral models (Kahneman & Tversky), sustainable finance, ESG, financial innovation Statistics, econometrics, psychology, environmental science

Concept, Definition, Theory, Method

  • Concept:
    • The building blocks of any theory or model
    • The abstraction representing an object
    • The property of an object or a certain phenomenon
    • The foundation of communication of certain object and idea
    • Introduce perspective, a way of viewing certain object at the empirical world
    • Example: return, price, value, cost, income, liquidity, depreciation
  • Definition:
    • The clarification and precision of concepts are achieved through definitions
    • Conceptual definition vs operational definition
    • Conceptual definition is a definition that describe a concept using other concepts, point out unique attributes of the concept
    • Conceptual definition would be in the literature review i.e. chapter 2 of our research paper
    • Example of conceptual definition: stock market is an exchange where stockbrokers and traders can buy and sell equity stock
    • Operational definition is a set of procedures describing the activities to be performed to empirically
    • Operational definition would be in the methodology section i.e. chapter 3 of our research paper
    • Example of operational definition: stock market size is the total market capitalization which is the market aggregate of multiplication of number of stock outstanding and market price of stock traded

Empirical Step on Financial Research

Empirical Step on Financial Research<

  • Problem spesification Formulation of economic and business questions of interest. A light literature review should be conducted at this stage. The most important issue that needs to be determined is the phenomena under scrutiny.
  • Methodology selection After determining the phenomena, the subsequent stage is the collection and analysis of relevant statistical data through an in-depth literature review. The following questions should be answered in this stage:
    • What variables that are involved, define whether the variable is exogenous or endogenous
    • Are there any theories and economic models that explain the phenomena
    • How should the analysis be and what kind of data and statistical techniques are relevant
    • For instance, we know from previous literature that the price of an asset is influenced by the return of a risk-free instrument and the risk premium, i.e., the economic model is V=f(return, risk)
  • Empirical Modelling The economic model should be exactly specified (e.g., type of relationship between variables) and should be operationally defined (e.g., what is the proxy of certain concepts). The example of an econometric model for the aforementioned economic model is V_{i,t}=\beta_{1}.\textrm{Risk Free}+\beta_{2}.\textrm{Market Premium}+\beta_{3}.\textrm{Size Discount}+\upsilon_{i,t}
  • Analysis Choosing proper statistical techniques, for instance: ordinary least square (OLS). This step also includes answering the following questions:
    • Whether algebra notation in the hypothesis is supported by parameter estimation
    • How fit the model as implied from the goodness of fit measure
    • Whether any specification problem in the model, for instance, omitted variable
    • Whether the assumption of the estimator is fulfilled
  • Application The use of model for previously supposed objective
