History of Finance Reserach

Timeline Themes Paradigm Foundation
The Old Finance Analysis of financial statements and the nature of financial claims Security analysis (Graham & Dodd), the financial policy of corporations (Dewing) Accounting and law
The Modern Finance Valuation based on rational economic behavior Optimization (Markowitz), irrelevance of capital structure (Miller & Modigliani), irrelevance of dividend policy (Modigliani & Miller), CAPM (Sharpe, Lintner, Mossin), efficient market (Fama) Financial economics
The New Finance Inefficient market Inductive ad hoc expected return (Haugen), factor models (Chen, Roll, & Ross), behavioral models (Kahneman & Tversky), sustainable finance, ESG, financial innovation Statistics, econometrics, psychology, environmental science

Source: Haugen (1994)

Important Research in Finance
Decade Topic and Research
1950s Portfoliio theory (Markowitz, 1953); capital structure irrelevance (Modigliani & Miller, 1958)
1970s Capital Asset Pricing Model (Sharpe, 1964; Lintner, 1965; Mossin, 1966)
1970s Efficient Market Hypothesis (Fama, 1970; Fama, 1991); option pricing model (Black & Scholes, 1973); continuous time (Merton, 1973; Merton, 1990), Arbitrage Pricing Theory (Ross, 1976); agency theory (Jensen & Meckling, 1976); incentive signalling (Ross, 1977; Bhattacharya, 1976); term structure of interest rates (Cox, Ingersoll, & Ross, 1977), martingale pricing approach (Harrison & Pliska, 1979)
1980s futures pricing (Cox, Ingersoll, & Ross, 1981), pecking-order theory (Myers & Majluf, 1984), agency cost of free cash flow (Jensen, 1986)
